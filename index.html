Patricia Saidu Memorial Scholarship
Patricia Saidu Memorial Health Fellows Scholarship
Sixth Annual - Baton Rouge Youth Coalition

This is an annual BRYC-exclusive scholarship renamed in honor of Patricia Saidu, who embodied everything it means to be a Health Fellow before her life was tragically cut short. The Patricia Saidu Health Fellows Scholarship will commemorate Patricia annually, uplifting her inspiring legacy, honoring her family (who blessed the renaming), and serving as the emotional core of this initiative.

This scholarship helps to strengthen our healthcare workforce pipeline while offering life-changing early-career exposure to underserved youth. A recent McKinsey & Company report projects a global shortage of at least 10 million healthcare workers by 2030, forcing clinics and hospitals into more innovative talent-creation models. Meanwhile, new Bain & Company research shows that young people who engage in meaningful career awareness, networking, mentoring, and work-based learning in high school and college achieve significantly better career outcomes.

Through the Health Fellows Program, we are breaking down financial barriers that prevent lower-income students from pursuing healthcare careers. We are grateful for our partnership with the Baton Rouge Clinic and other partners such as the BCBSLA Foundation and FranU, which has allowed us to effect tangible and immediate change. Together, we are building equity in healthcare at both the patient and provider levels. Our partnership began in 2020 and is only continuing!

Your Charge as a Reviewer
1
Read the full essay before scoring. Resist the urge to skim. The rubric rewards depth, and depth often reveals itself slowly. These students worked hard on these essays.
2
Score what is there, not what is missing. These are students at different stages - a high schooler and an alumni fellow will naturally write very differently. Evaluate each against what you would expect for where they are in their journey.
3
Be consistent. Try to hold the same standard across all applicants in a category. If you would give one student a 3 for Purpose, make sure you are applying that same bar to everyone.
4
Look for purpose over polish. We are identifying students who show genuine commitment to a health career, not just strong writers. A student who clearly knows why they want to serve their community matters more than a perfectly structured essay.
5
Your scores are confidential. Reviewers do not see each other's scores during the process. Only the admin sees aggregate results. Score honestly and independently.
6
When in doubt, trust the rubric. The four categories - Interest, Engagement, Purpose, and Writing Mechanics - are designed to surface the whole student, not just their writing ability. Let the criteria guide you.
